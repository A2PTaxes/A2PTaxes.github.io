Client Onboarding

Welcome! Whether you’re filing taxes, starting a business, or seeking guidance, this page will walk you through what to expect and how to prepare. My goal is to make the process clear, calm, and accessible for every client.

How Appointments Work

  • Appointments are mobile, virtual, or in-person depending on your needs.
  • Sessions are paced to match your processing style — slower pacing is always welcome.
  • Most appointments last 45–60 minutes, with a minimum of two appointments per return... but complex returns may require more interaction than that.
  • You’ll receive a summary of next steps after each appointment.

Response Time Expectations

  • Messages are answered within 2 business days.
  • During peak tax season, response times may be slightly longer.
  • Portal messages are prioritized over email for security reasons.

What to Bring

Here’s a general list of documents that may apply to your situation:

  • Two forms of ID, one of which should be a government‑issued photo ID
  • Social Security cards for all individuals on the return
  • Last return you filed
  • W‑2s, 1099s, and income statements
  • 1098 forms (mortgage interest, tuition, etc.)
  • Business income and expense records
  • Childcare provider information
  • Health insurance forms (1095‑A, if applicable)
  • Any IRS or state letters you’ve received

If you’re unsure what applies to you, don’t worry — we’ll go over it together.

Secure Portal Access

All tax documents must be uploaded through the secure MyTaxOffice portal. After your first appointment or inquiry, you’ll receive a portal link with instructions.

  • Do not email tax documents or sensitive information.
  • You can upload photos if you don’t have a scanner.
  • If you need help using the portal, I’m happy to walk you through it.

Prior‑Year Returns & POA

If you need help with a prior‑year return and no longer have your documents:

  • You will first attempt to retrieve transcripts through IRS.gov and/or your state Revenue Department.
  • If you are unable to access them, I can request them on your behalf using a Power of Attorney (POA).
  • POA is used only as a last resort and only with your explicit consent.

Accessibility & Accommodations

I welcome clients of all backgrounds, abilities, genders, faiths, and processing styles. If you need accommodations — such as slower pacing, written summaries, visual aids, or alternative meeting locations — please let me know. I’m happy to adjust.

After Your Appointment

  • You’ll receive a summary of next steps.
  • You’ll be notified when your return is ready for review and signature.
  • Once signed, your return will be e‑filed and you’ll receive confirmation.
