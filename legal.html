Legal & Privacy Information

Ames 2 Please Taxes is committed to transparency, ethical practice, and the protection of client information. The following statements outline important legal and privacy information related to our services.

General Information Disclaimer

Information provided on this website, through workshops, or in general conversation is for educational purposes only and should not be considered personalized tax, legal, or financial advice. Each taxpayer’s situation is unique, and formal advice can only be given after reviewing your specific documents and circumstances.

Privacy & Data Security

We take your privacy seriously. Personally identifiable information (PII), tax documents, and sensitive data should only be submitted through the secure MyTaxOffice portal. We do not accept tax documents via email, text message, or social media.

Client information is never sold, shared, or disclosed except as required by law or with explicit client authorization.

Document Uploads & Communication

Clients are responsible for ensuring that all documents uploaded to the secure portal are accurate and complete. Email communication should be limited to general questions only. Sensitive information must not be sent via email.

Record Retention Policy

We retain client tax records for a minimum of three years, in accordance with IRS and Oregon Board of Tax Practitioners guidelines. After the initial three-year period, documents are reviewed and may be securely destroyed or retained for up to a total of seven years.

Decisions about retaining documents beyond three years are based on anticipated future filing needs and applicable laws at that time. Clients are encouraged to keep their own copies of all tax returns and supporting documents, as this is required by law.

Oregon Licensing & Compliance

Ames 2 Please Taxes operates under Oregon law and is licensed by the Oregon Board of Tax Practitioners. As an Oregon Licensed Tax Consultant (LTC#1922362‑C) and IRS Electronic Return Originator (ERO), we follow all state and federal regulations regarding tax preparation, data security, and ethical practice.

Electronic Signatures & E‑File Authorization

By signing electronically through the secure portal, clients authorize the preparation and electronic filing of their tax return. Clients are responsible for reviewing all documents before signing and notifying us of any errors or omissions.

Website Cookies & Functionality

This website may use basic cookies to support navigation and functionality. No tracking, advertising, or analytics cookies are used.

Updates to This Page

This legal and privacy information may be updated periodically to reflect changes in law, technology, or business practices. The most current version will always be available on this page.
